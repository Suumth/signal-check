# Smallest Real Anchor

## Decision

Run a small real-world comprehension test before building.

## Target Segment

People who already use a thermometer, probe, or smart grill for meat cooks.

## Concrete Action

Show two short SearCue descriptions and one generic negative control to five real cooks. Ask them to describe when they would use it, what they would not trust, and what they currently do instead.

## Success Evidence

At least three people name an active cooking moment where short cues would reduce uncertainty.

## Stop Evidence

Most people say existing thermometer alerts already cover the need, or they object to trusting software during the cook.

## Time Box

One week.
