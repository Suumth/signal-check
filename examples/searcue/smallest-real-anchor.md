# Smallest Real Anchor

## Decision

Run a small real-world comprehension and reachability test before building.

## Target Segment

People who already use GrillEye, ThermoPro, Inkbird, or similar Bluetooth thermometers for meat cooks. These manufacturer names are only neutral compatibility contexts, not claimed partners or official integrations.

## Concrete Action

Contact 8 to 12 reachable Bluetooth thermometer users through direct outreach, beta-interest channels, or cooking communities. Show two SearCue descriptions, one neutral compatibility note, and one negative control. Ask them to describe:

- the exact grill moment they think the app helps with
- what they currently do for target temperature, pull timing, rest timing, reconnect, and history
- what they would not trust
- whether manufacturer names imply anything they should not imply
- whether they would reply to a beta invite for a manual prototype test

## Success Evidence

At least four people name a concrete active-cook moment where cues could reduce uncertainty, and at least two are reachable for a beta reply or short follow-up. No one should interpret manufacturer names as partnership, sponsorship, or official support.

## Stop Evidence

Most people say existing thermometer alerts already cover the need, interpret the copy as a food-safety guarantee, assume official manufacturer support, or object to trusting software during the cook.

## Time Box

One week.

## What This Does Not Prove

This does not prove demand, purchase intent, food-safety reliability, device compatibility, or manufacturer support.
