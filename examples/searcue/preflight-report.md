# SearCue Preflight Report

## Summary

The example suggests the concept is easiest to understand when tied to existing thermometer workflows. It should not be framed as cooking authority. The main uncertainty is whether real cooks want cues during an active session or prefer prep-stage guidance.

This report is an example artifact. It does not validate the idea.

## What To Keep

- Hardware companion framing.
- Short cue language.
- Clear boundary that cues assist rather than decide.

## What To Change

- Replace broad smart-food language with concrete cooking moments.
- Clarify device assumptions.
- Test whether "during the cook" is the right moment.

## What To Stop

- Do not use generic platform positioning.
- Do not imply reliable cooking outcomes.
- Do not infer willingness to pay.

## Negative Control Readout

The generic control was less clear than concrete SearCue copy. That supports keeping the stimulus specific, but it is not proof of demand.

## Kill Criteria Result

The example passes the "concrete cooking moment" criterion directionally, but trust and redundancy with existing alerts remain unresolved.
