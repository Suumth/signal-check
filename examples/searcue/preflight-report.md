# SearCue Preflight Report

## Summary

The example suggests the concept is easiest to understand when tied to concrete Bluetooth thermometer moments: target temperature, pull timing, heat checks, rest timing, reconnect, and history. It should not be framed as cooking authority, food-safety assurance, or manufacturer-backed software.

The main uncertainty is whether real Bluetooth thermometer users want guidance during the cook, or whether existing device alerts and manual timers already cover enough of the job.

This report is an example artifact. It does not validate the idea.

## What To Keep

- Hardware companion framing.
- Short cue language.
- Clear boundary that cues assist rather than decide.
- Concrete grill moments instead of broad smart-food language.

## What To Change

- Replace broad smart-food language with concrete cooking moments.
- Clarify device assumptions and compatibility boundaries.
- State that GrillEye, ThermoPro, and Inkbird are neutral beta or compatibility contexts only.
- Test whether "during the cook" is the right moment.

## What To Stop

- Do not use generic platform positioning.
- Do not imply reliable cooking outcomes.
- Do not infer willingness to pay.
- Do not imply partnership, sponsorship, official integration, or food-safety guarantees.

## Negative Control Readout

The generic control was less clear than concrete SearCue copy. The partnership control produced trust friction because official-brand and guarantee language created stronger claims than the method allows.

This supports keeping compatibility language bounded. It is not proof of demand.

## Kill Criteria Result

The example passes the "concrete cooking moment" criterion directionally. It does not pass enough to justify building. Trust, compatibility assumptions, food-safety boundaries, and redundancy with existing alerts remain unresolved.

## Prompt Robustness Readout

Findings should be treated as stable only if they hold across neutral short copy, compatibility-with-names, and compatibility-without-names variants. Any finding that depends on official-sounding manufacturer language should be discarded.
