# SearCue Brief

## Name

SearCue

## One-Sentence Description

SearCue is an iPhone app concept for people using Bluetooth grill thermometers. It helps them understand concrete grill moments: target temperature, when to pull from the grill, heat checks, resting time, reconnect state, and temperature history.

## Example Role

This is a Signal Check example case and method stress test. It is not the Signal Check project itself, and it is not evidence that SearCue should be built.

## Target Segment

Hardware companion app users who already cook with probes, thermometers, or smart grills.

## Problem

During an active cook, users often need timing guidance at the exact moment they are busy handling heat, food, and guests.

## Proposed Solution

A companion app layer that gives short, context-aware cues for target temperature, pulling from heat, checking heat, resting, reconnecting, and reviewing the cook history.

## Compatibility Context

GrillEye, ThermoPro, and Inkbird may be mentioned only as neutral beta or compatibility contexts for Bluetooth thermometer users.

This example does not imply:

- manufacturer partnership
- sponsorship
- official integration
- criticism of any manufacturer
- food-safety guarantees
- validated demand

## Current Alternatives

- Built-in thermometer app alerts
- Recipe instructions
- YouTube or blog guidance
- Personal experience
- Manual notes or screenshots

## Riskiest Assumption

Users want contextual help during the cook, not only temperature visibility or prep-stage recipe guidance.

## Kill Criteria

- If fitting segments see the idea as redundant with existing thermometer alerts, narrow the wedge.
- If trust concerns dominate, do not frame the product as authoritative cooking advice.
- If people expect food-safety guarantees or manufacturer-backed accuracy, narrow the positioning.
- If the app cannot end with a small real-world test, stop the preflight.
