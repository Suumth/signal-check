# Smallest Real Anchor: Two-Audience Homepage
## Real audience
8-12 real readers total:
- 4-6 people who match the practical proof-product audience
- 4-6 people who match the professional credibility visitor audience
## Real artifact
Two or three lightweight homepage wireframes or text-only homepage outlines:
1. lab frame + proof product early + method behind it
2. proof product first
3. method or brand first
## Real questions
Ask each person:
- What do you think this page is for?
- What is the concrete product or proof?
- Who do you think this is for?
- What feels credible?
- What feels vague?
- What would you click next?
- What would you expect to see before trusting it?
## Observable signal
Continue with the story architecture if most readers can explain:
- the concrete proof product
- the broader lab frame
- why the method is relevant
- what the page wants them to do next
## Revise rule
Revise if:
- product buyers only remember the product and miss the lab
- professional visitors only see a portfolio
- readers describe the page as an AI studio
- the method section feels abstract or self-important
## Stop rule
Stop or reframe if:
- readers cannot explain what exists
- both target groups interpret the page differently in incompatible ways
- the proof product does not make the broader lab more credible
- the strongest version still feels vague
## Safety note
This real anchor is a proposed next test. It does not prove demand, validate purchase intent, or replace user research.
