# Product Brief: Two-Audience Homepage
## Product idea
A small independent product lab wants a homepage that explains its work clearly.
The homepage has to serve two audiences:
1. practical buyers who may care about a concrete proof product, and
2. professional visitors who are evaluating the builder's product judgment, method, and credibility.
## Run question
Which homepage story structure is clearest without making the site feel like either a single-product landing page or a vague portfolio?
## Target user hypothesis
The homepage needs to be understandable to:
- people who mainly care about the concrete proof product
- professional visitors who mainly care about product quality, method, and judgment
## Problem hypothesis
If the homepage starts too abstractly, the lab feels vague.
If the homepage starts only with the proof product, the broader product-lab frame disappears.
If the homepage starts with methodology, the page can feel academic or self-referential.
## Current alternative
The homepage could be written as:
- a brand-first product lab page
- a proof-product-first page
- a method-first page
- a personal portfolio
- an AI studio page
## Product promise
The homepage should make the product lab's practical competence visible through concrete product proof, restrained language, and clear method boundaries.
## Risky claims to avoid
- "validated by users"
- "market-proven method"
- "AI market research"
- "guaranteed product success"
- "everyone understands this instantly"
- synthetic reactions presented as user feedback
## Intended Signal Check decision
Choose the clearest homepage story architecture to test with real readers.
