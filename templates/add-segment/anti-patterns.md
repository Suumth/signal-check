# Segment Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- named fictional people
- demographic trivia without product relevance
- aspirational buyer descriptions
- only-positive target users
- segments that mirror the founder
- segments invented to make the product look useful
- vague groups such as everyone, busy professionals, or modern teams
- segments with no current workaround
- segments with no likely objection
- segments with no realistic way to reach real people
- segments that cannot disagree with the idea
- segments treated as representative of a market
- synthetic reactions treated as testimonials
