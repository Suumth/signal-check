# Example: Quiet Invoice Mini Run

This is a public-safe style reference for the optional Signal Check Run Assistant. It is intentionally small and generic.

## Product idea

Quiet Invoice helps freelancers write calm, clear invoice follow-up messages without sounding aggressive or apologetic.

## Product brief excerpt

Target assumption: freelancers who dislike chasing overdue invoices may need a lightweight way to draft firm but calm follow-up messages.

Problem/job: sending reminders that protect the relationship while still making payment expectations clear.

Current evidence: common anecdotal pain, but no validated demand.

Kill criteria:

- users already have a preferred wording system
- the tool creates legal or relationship risk
- users want full accounting automation instead

Smallest Real Anchor candidate: ask five freelancers to rewrite one real but anonymized overdue-invoice scenario and compare whether the tool's draft would save time or reduce discomfort.

## Example segment mix

- Fitting: solo freelancer with recurring late payments
- Adjacent: small studio with admin support
- Skeptical: freelancer worried that templates sound generic
- Poor fit: enterprise finance team with formal collections workflow
- Non-market control: someone who never invoices clients

## Example negative control

Overclaiming message: "Quiet Invoice gets clients to pay faster."

Expected reaction: skepticism, because payment speed cannot be promised from wording alone.

Failure condition: synthetic reactions accept this claim without objection.

## Example claim-safe synthesis line

The run suggests that the strongest question is not whether freelancers want an invoice tool, but whether calm wording help is valuable enough as a standalone workflow before accounting automation becomes the expected solution.

## Example Smallest Real Anchor

Test: Share three anonymized invoice follow-up drafts with five freelancers who have sent at least one payment reminder in the last 90 days.

Observable signal: they choose one draft they would actually adapt and explain what they would change.

Threshold: continue only if at least three participants can name a specific scenario where the draft saves time or reduces discomfort.

Kill criterion: stop or reposition if most participants say the draft feels generic, risky, or unnecessary compared with their current approach.

## Boundary

This example does not validate demand or represent freelancer behavior. It demonstrates file shape and claim-safe tone.
