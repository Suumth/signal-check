# Run Review Rubric

## Purpose

The Run Review Rubric evaluates the quality of a Signal Check run.

It does not evaluate whether the product idea is good. It evaluates whether the run was disciplined, skeptical, claim-safe, and useful for deciding the next real test.

## Scoring scale

Use the following scale for each criterion:

- `0` — missing or unsafe
- `1` — present but weak
- `2` — usable
- `3` — strong

Avoid false precision. The score is a review aid, not a measurement system.

## Criteria

### 1. Product brief clarity

Score `3` when the audience hypothesis is clear, the problem or job is explicit, the proposition is understandable, uncertainty is acknowledged, and claims are bounded.

Score `0` when the brief is mostly pitch language.

### 2. Segment composition

Score `3` when likely-fit, adjacent, skeptical, and poor-fit or non-market control perspectives exist and are context-based rather than fake personas.

Score `0` when all segments are variants of likely buyers.

### 3. Stimulus design

Score `3` when stimuli are meaningfully different, at least one stimulus is skeptical or control-like, no stimulus implies proof of demand, and language is understandable but not over-polished.

Score `0` when all stimuli are promotional claims.

### 4. Negative controls

Score `3` when controls test hype, vagueness, wrong audience, or unsupported claims; controls are interpreted explicitly; and passing controls trigger caution.

Score `0` when no controls exist.

### 5. Reaction integrity

Score `3` when reactions allow rejection, confusion is preserved, objections are not smoothed away, no testimonial language is used, and no real user attribution appears.

Score `0` when reactions are written as customer quotes or proof.

### 6. Bias audit quality

Score `3` when response collapse, prompt sensitivity, missing counter-evidence, overly agreeable tone, and negative-control failure are checked.

Score `0` when no bias audit exists.

### 7. Synthesis quality

Score `3` when synthesis includes what worked, what failed, what remained unclear, differences by segment, negative-control interpretation, uncertainty, and limits.

Score `0` when it reads like validation.

### 8. Claim safety

Score `3` when the run explicitly avoids representative claims, validated demand claims, purchase-intent claims, market-size claims, and user-research substitution claims.

Score `0` when it claims the idea is validated.

### 9. Decision usefulness

Score `3` when the decision is one of continue, revise, narrow, clarify, pause, or kill; is tied to evidence and counter-evidence; and points to a concrete next step.

Score `0` when the decision is simply build it.

### 10. Smallest Real Anchor

Score `3` when the anchor defines real audience or channel, artifact or interaction, observable signal, threshold, kill or revise condition, and documentation plan.

Score `0` when the next step is another synthetic run only.

## Interpreting total score

- `24–30`: strong run; ready for real-world anchor
- `18–23`: usable but needs targeted revision
- `10–17`: weak; repeat key steps before acting
- `0–9`: unsafe or too self-confirming; do not use for decisions

Every review must end with:

```text
This run does / does not provide a disciplined basis for the next real test because...
```
