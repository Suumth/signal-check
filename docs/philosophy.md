# Philosophy

Signal Check exists to slow down premature building.

Most early product ideas fail long before implementation. The common failure is not that the team lacked code. It is that the team skipped the hard thinking: who is this for, what pain is being claimed, what competing behavior already exists, what would disconfirm the idea, and what real-world anchor should happen before more investment.

Signal Check is the project. Product Preflight is the workflow. The Synthetic Panel Kit is the method layer that uses segment files, prompts, templates, controls, and reports to make early assumptions visible.

v0.1 treats the method as the product. Files are plain, reviewable, and forkable. There is no CLI, API, Web UI, model integration, hidden engine, automated verdict, or automated scoring and grading engine.

Core principles:

- Segment-first, not persona-first.
- Free reactions before structured classification.
- Include fitting, adjacent, skeptical, and poor-fit perspectives.
- Include non-market controls when a poor-fit perspective should not support the idea.
- Add negative controls to expose over-reading.
- Check prompt robustness before trusting a pattern.
- Keep evidence, counter-evidence, and uncertainty together.
- Write kill criteria before interpreting signal.
- End every Product Preflight with a Smallest Real Anchor.

Signal Check is a discipline for better uncertainty, not a shortcut around it. It is not market research, not an AI oracle, not a fake-persona generator, and not a source of synthetic testimonials.
