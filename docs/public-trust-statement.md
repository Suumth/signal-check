# Public Trust Statement

## Core statement

Signal Check is an open-source method kit for thinking through early product ideas before building.

It helps users:

- clarify who an idea might be for
- surface likely objections
- compare messages
- identify weak assumptions
- design a small real-world next test

It does not:

- produce representative market research
- measure demand
- validate purchase intent
- replace interviews, surveys, analytics, usability testing, or sales evidence
- never generate real customer testimonials
- decide whether a product should be built

## Why this matters

Synthetic reactions can make an idea feel more certain than it is. Signal Check is designed to resist that failure mode.

The method intentionally includes skeptical, adjacent, poor-fit, and non-market-control perspectives. The goal is not to make an idea sound good. The goal is to expose where the idea is unclear, fragile, or untested.

## Public wording rule

Prefer:

> directional preflight
> structured skepticism
> better questions before building
> real-test planning

Avoid:

> AI market research
> virtual focus group
> validated demand
> real user proof
> synthetic customer evidence
