# Evidence Ladder

## Purpose

The Evidence Ladder keeps Signal Check outputs in their proper place.

Signal Check can improve thinking before research. It does not replace research, sales conversations, usability testing, or behavioral evidence.

## Ladder levels

### Level 0 — Internal belief

The team believes the idea is useful.

Evidence type:

- founder intuition
- internal debate
- anecdotal motivation

Risk:

- self-persuasion
- solution-first framing
- missing objections

Signal Check can help here by forcing a clearer product brief.

### Level 1 — Synthetic preflight

The idea is exposed to structured, non-persona perspectives.

Evidence type:

- segment reactions
- objections
- confusion points
- negative-control behavior
- prompt robustness checks

Risk:

- mistaking synthetic output for user evidence
- over-reading positive reactions
- prompt-led agreement

Signal Check primarily operates at this level.

### Level 2 — Expert or peer review

A real person with relevant experience critiques the idea or method.

Evidence type:

- expert review
- founder peer feedback
- domain critique
- technical feasibility review

Risk:

- authority bias
- reviewer not matching the target context
- politeness bias

### Level 3 — Real exposure

The idea or message is shown to reachable real people in the target context.

Evidence type:

- comprehension interviews
- message tests
- cold outreach replies
- community feedback
- beta-interest screening

Risk:

- vanity metrics
- weak targeting
- leading questions

### Level 4 — Intentional action

People take a meaningful action that costs time, attention, reputation, or effort.

Evidence type:

- booking a call
- joining a qualified beta
- bringing a current workaround
- trying a concierge workflow
- importing real data

Risk:

- novelty curiosity
- one-off politeness
- low commitment

### Level 5 — Behavioral or commercial commitment

People repeatedly use, pay for, deploy, or depend on the product.

Evidence type:

- paid conversion
- retention
- usage under real constraints
- renewal
- referral with context
- workflow dependency

Risk:

- early adopter distortion
- channel-specific effects
- incomplete product-market understanding

## What Signal Check can support

Signal Check can support statements like:

- “This idea has visible assumption risks.”
- “These segments would likely interpret the message differently.”
- “This positioning appears clearer than the negative control in this preflight run.”
- “The next real test should focus on compatibility, trust, or comprehension.”

## What Signal Check must not support

Signal Check must not support statements like:

- “Users want this.”
- “The market validated this.”
- “This proves demand.”
- “This is statistically significant.”
- “The target segment will buy.”
- “Synthetic responses are equivalent to user research.”

## Evidence handoff rule

Every synthesis should end with:

```text
This run only supports preflight learning. The next evidence step is: [Smallest Real Anchor].
```

## Kill criteria

A run should define what would stop or redirect the idea.

Examples:

- Target people cannot explain the message back in their own words.
- The strongest objection cannot be answered without a claim the team cannot support.
- The only positive reactions come from poor-fit or novelty-seeking segments.
- The negative control performs as well as the intended message.
- No reachable audience can be identified for the next real test.
